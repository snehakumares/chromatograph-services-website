Sample System Fault-Finding Basics

A troubleshooting framework focused on the sample path rather than the analyser electronics.

Discuss an issueBack to Knowledge Hub

Why the sample system deserves first attention

A large share of analyser problems originate in sample presentation, not in the electronics or detector.

What to check first

Check pressure control, flow, filtration, venting, phase condition, contamination risk, and whether the sample path is actually aligned as expected.

Why symptoms can mislead

A sample system fault may present as drift, instability, poor repeatability, or apparent detector trouble.

Why simple observations matter

Visual condition, drain status, regulator behaviour, tubing temperature, and recent interventions often reveal more than software screens alone.

Why this improves troubleshooting speed

Teams that check the sample system early usually avoid unnecessary replacement of healthy analytical components.

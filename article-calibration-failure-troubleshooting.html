Calibration Failure Troubleshooting

How to think through calibration failure without defaulting immediately to detector replacement.

Discuss an issueBack to Knowledge Hub

Why calibration failure deserves structure

Calibration failure is a symptom, not a diagnosis. The reason may lie in the gas, the sample path, the detector, the timing, or the input assumptions.

Start with the obvious but meaningful checks

Confirm calibration gas identity, bottle condition, pressure, switching path, recent maintenance, and any method or configuration changes.

Why repeatability matters here

If the analyser cannot repeat itself on the reference gas, the problem is often more fundamental than the calibration result alone suggests.

What deeper causes can include

Timing drift, contamination, valve condition, utility instability, or detector issues can all produce calibration failure.

Why disciplined records help

A useful history of calibration outcomes makes it much easier to distinguish a sudden event from a slow decline.

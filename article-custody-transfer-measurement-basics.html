Custody Transfer Measurement Basics

Why commercial gas measurement demands more rigour than ordinary process indication.

Discuss an issueBack to Knowledge Hub

What custody transfer means

Custody transfer is the point where gas ownership or value is determined using measured quantity and quality.

Why the standard is higher

Because money and contractual performance depend on the result, the measurement system must be accurate, traceable, maintained, and defensible.

What forms the chain

The chain usually includes flow measurement, pressure and temperature inputs, gas composition, property calculation, and reporting logic.

Why uncertainty matters

Small biases can have large commercial impact over time. Good custody transfer systems focus on systematic measurement quality, not only day-to-day operability.

Why this matters beyond metering specialists

Many analyser and chromatograph decisions influence custody transfer quality even when they are made outside the metering team.

Real Problems We Solve

Real problems engineers encounter with gas chromatographs and analyser systems.

High-priority issue

Unstable or drifting readings

Unexpected changes in analyser readings can affect process control, reporting confidence, and day-to-day decision-making. These issues may originate from detector instability, sample pressure changes, contamination, process effects, or sample system behaviour that is not immediately obvious from the analyser alone.

Detector instability Pressure variation Contamination risk Sample system behaviour

Calibration issues

Failed repeatability, inconsistent response factors, or unstable validation results that indicate deeper system problems.

Sample handling issues

Pressure regulation faults, liquid carryover, contamination, filtration issues, or poor sample conditioning affecting reliability.

Application issues

Configuration, method, or application mismatch problems that can lead to poor measurement performance even when hardware appears healthy.

Hardware reliability issues

Valve wear, detector degradation, electronics faults, and ageing components that gradually affect stability and performance.

Operation and maintenance challenges

Recurring issues driven by maintenance gaps, limited procedures, inconsistent practices, or lack of practical operating knowledge.

Commissioning and recommissioning issues

Problems that appear after startup, shutdown work, or system changes, where settings, timing, sample conditions, or practical field checks have not yet stabilised.

Remote Support

Discuss your GC or analyser issue with a specialist

Many gas chromatograph and analyser issues can often be clarified quickly through a technical discussion.

In many cases, the problem is related to configuration, operating conditions, or minor adjustments rather than requiring immediate hardware intervention.

Remote support allows engineers and technicians to review the symptoms, discuss recent changes, and identify practical checks that may help restore stable operation. Even when a site visit is eventually required, an early technical discussion can help narrow the investigation and save valuable time.

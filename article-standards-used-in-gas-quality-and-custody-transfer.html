Standards Used in Gas Quality and Custody Transfer

An overview of why recognised standards are central to trusted gas quality, flow, and energy measurement.

Discuss an issue Back to Knowledge Hub

Why standards are necessary

Gas quality and custody transfer measurement involve both technical and commercial consequences. Standards provide a common framework so that all parties understand how values are measured, calculated, and verified.

Without standards, consistent agreement becomes much harder to achieve.

Different standards serve different parts of the chain

Some standards govern composition-based property calculations, others govern flow measurement, and others define calibration, uncertainty, installation, or verification practices. Together they support the full path from sample to reported result.

No single standard covers every part of the system.

Why traceability matters

Standards support traceability by making it clear how a result was produced and against what recognised method it should be assessed. This is important for performance review, auditability, and dispute reduction.

A traceable result is easier to defend and easier to improve.

Standards do not replace engineering judgment

A compliant calculation or installation can still perform poorly if the sample is unrepresentative or the design is weak. Standards create the framework, but sound engineering still determines whether the system works well in practice.

Good measurement needs both compliance and competence.

A practical approach

Users should know which standard applies to which part of the chain, where site procedures extend or interpret those requirements, and how operating practice supports ongoing compliance.

Understanding the standard is useful; applying it well is what protects the measurement.

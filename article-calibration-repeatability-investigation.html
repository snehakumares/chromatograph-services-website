Calibration Repeatability Investigation

A structured case-study style look at how unstable calibration results are investigated and narrowed down.

Discuss an issue Back to Knowledge Hub

Situation

A gas chromatograph began showing inconsistent calibration behaviour even though the calibration routine itself completed successfully. Consecutive checks did not provide the level of repeatability expected for a stable system.

The issue reduced confidence in both analyser condition and reported results.

Observed symptoms

Response movement was not fully random, but it was not stable enough to support confidence. Chromatograms also showed small differences between runs that suggested the issue was affecting the physical analysis rather than only the final calculation output.

The evidence pointed to a real repeatability problem, not only a reporting issue.

Investigation direction

The review considered calibration gas delivery, sample and carrier stability, injection repeatability, and chromatogram consistency. Historical comparison against previously stable behaviour helped narrow the likely fault area.

Looking at patterns over time was essential.

Likely cause

The instability was most consistent with a repeatability issue in the analytical chain rather than a single isolated software or reporting fault. In this type of case, the root cause is often found in injection consistency, contamination, or utility stability.

The calibration only revealed the weakness; it did not create it.

Practical lesson

When calibration repeatability degrades, the investigation should go beyond the pass or fail result and examine the physical behaviour behind it. Calibration is one of the best windows into deeper analyser health.

Repeatability problems deserve structured evidence review, not guesswork.

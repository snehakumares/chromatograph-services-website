Hydrocarbon Dew Point Basics

A practical introduction to hydrocarbon dew point and why it matters in gas sampling and measurement systems.

Discuss an issueBack to Knowledge Hub

What hydrocarbon dew point means

Hydrocarbon dew point describes the conditions at which heavier hydrocarbons begin to condense from the gas.

Why it matters to sampling

If the sample passes through conditions where liquid forms, the gas reaching the analyser may no longer represent the process stream correctly.

Why pressure changes are important

Pressure reduction can push the sample toward condensation even when the process gas looked stable upstream.

Why this connects to measurement reliability

Dew point issues affect sample systems, chromatographs, metering credibility, and contamination risk.

Why operators should understand it

A basic grasp of dew point behaviour helps explain many recurring sample system and analyser issues that otherwise seem unrelated.

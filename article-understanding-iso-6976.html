Understanding ISO 6976 in Natural Gas Energy Measurement

A practical explanation of how gas composition becomes heating value, density, and related energy properties.

Discuss an issue Back to Knowledge Hub

What ISO 6976 is used for

ISO 6976 provides standard calculation methods for deriving natural gas properties from composition. These properties include calorific value, relative density, and Wobbe index, which are central to energy measurement and gas quality assessment.

The standard converts composition into commercially useful properties.

Why composition matters first

The calculation starts with a measured gas composition, typically in mole percent. Each component contributes differently to the energy content and physical properties of the final gas mixture.

That is why good composition measurement is the foundation of good energy calculation.

What properties are typically calculated

Common outputs include gross and net calorific value, relative density, molar mass, and Wobbe index. These values are used in billing, operational control, interchangeability review, and wider gas quality evaluation.

The composition itself is only the beginning of the measurement chain.

Why standardisation matters

Using a recognised standard ensures that buyers, sellers, operators, and laboratories are speaking the same technical language. Without a common basis, the same gas could be valued differently by different methods.

Standardisation supports both fairness and traceability.

What users should remember

The calculation standard can only work correctly when the input composition is representative, complete, and properly maintained. A precise formula cannot rescue a poor sample or poor analysis.

Strong energy measurement always combines good sampling, good analysis, and correct calculation.
